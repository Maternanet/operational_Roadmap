The Problem

Three compounding failures, one preventable crisis
In Africa, approximately 530 mothers die every day during pregnancy or childbirth — more than malaria, HIV, and tuberculosis combined. With 454 maternal deaths per 100,000 live births, Africa's rate is 5× the global average. Up to 65% of high-risk pregnancies go undetected until an emergency occurs. The problem is not absence of care — it is absence of intelligence at the moment care is delivered.
Clinical Failure
Late detection

65% of high-risk pregnancies remain invisible until emergency presentation. The Three-Delays framework identifies decision, reach, and receipt as the dominant mortality drivers — all addressable at the community level before the facility emergency referrals.

Systemic Failure
Unnecessary burden

For every 1,000 pregnancies, at least 150 escalate to obstetric emergency care — which costs 4.5× more than a normal birth. Unnecessary referrals from undetected low-risk pregnancies clog tertiary hospitals and consume resources from genuinely critical cases.

Economic Failure
Catastrophic cost

Over 85% of maternal healthcare costs in rural areas are paid out-of-pocket. When emergencies strike, families sell assets or borrow at predatory rates — pushing households into poverty precisely when a new life should bring opportunity. Every maternal death prevented generates $50,000+ in economic value.

The sequence is non-negotiable.

Collection architecture: Clinical variables are captured by the CHP at every visit via the mobile tool (Bluetooth BP cuff, MUAC tape, pulse oximeter — all synced to app). Socioeconomic variables are collected once at registration via a 5-minute USSD intake or voice-guided form — not at every visit. Environmental variables are pre-loaded by ward from county DHIS2 data and updated quarterly. No single visit captures all 85 variables; the record builds over the pregnancy arc. Completeness target: ≥80% of critical fields by third ANC contact.
Pillar 2 · Connect
Digital Doulas — the Data & Action Network
CHPs are not data collectors. They are care coordinators who generate data as a byproduct of genuine service.
Offline-first mobile tool — structured ANC visit guide, auto-risk flagging, Bluetooth vitals capture, offline sync. Auto-generates DHIS2 ANC register — the paperwork is done for them.
Community-clinic linkage — when the CWHI™ flags high-risk, the CHP receives an automated nudge. Referral is logged, facility is notified, outcome is tracked. The loop closes.
Edge computing architecture — ML prediction algorithms cached locally. Operates at <1kb/s. 85% model accuracy maintained offline. Patent application filed: Kenya Patent Office #2024/789.
Multi-modal mother interface — USSD for 2G / feature phones, Android app for smartphones, SMS nudges for all. Weekly pregnancy tips, danger sign checklist, appointment reminders. Mother's relationship is with Maternanet directly, not only through her CHP.
DHIS2 + SHA integration — auto-submission to Kenya's national health data system (DHIS2) and the Social Health Authority (SHA) for claims matching. Every SHA-covered birth creates a matched outcome record — the gold standard for longitudinal validation.
Supervisor dashboard — CHAs (CHP supervisors) see real-time CHP performance, visit compliance, and risk flag response rates. Recognition and incentive system tied to quality scores, not just visit counts.
Pillar 3 · Finance
Economic Resilience — Care Now, Lipa Later
The micro-savings wallet is not a financing feature. It is the postpartum re-engagement mechanism that closes the longitudinal record.
Critical safeguard applied: Previous deployments triggered credit disbursement directly from ML flag → CHP nudge → MFI loan. This creates a liability loop: at 81% precision, 1 in 5 flagged mothers is not clinically high-risk. A credit event attached to a false positive produces debt without clinical justification. The corrected protocol adds a mandatory clinical confirmation layer: AI flag → CHP visit → nurse-midwife or Clinical Officer confirmation at facility → credit authorisation. No financing is triggered without a skilled attendant's sign-off.
Micro-savings wallet
Mothers save incrementally from Kshs 30/week toward delivery costs. Milestone-linked: savings goals tied to ANC visit completion. This is the mechanism that drives postpartum re-engagement — the mother returns to check her savings balance and logs her postpartum data. The wallet earns its place in the data architecture, not just the impact story.
Care Now, Lipa Later (confirmed-risk only)
MFI credit lines for confirmed high-risk interventions. Confirmation required: Clinical Officer sign-off at facility, not CHP alone. At Phase 3 (1M profiles), our risk models price these loans more accurately than any MFI actuarial model — that is the commercial leverage, not the facilitation fee.
Maternal micro-insurance (Phase 3)
At 1M profiles with 18-month cohort depth: co-underwrite maternity insurance with Jubilee or APA. Our CWHI™ models give underwriters 30–40% better loss ratio than market pricing. Premium: Kshs 400–600/month. The data advantage is the product — not the insurance product itself.

CONSENT FLOW — FIRST VISIT REGISTRATION
01
Verbal consent in mother tongue

CHP reads a standardised 3-sentence consent statement in Kiswahili, Kikuyu, Dholuo, or Kalenjin (language determined during onboarding). Statement: what data is collected, who sees it, and how to stop. No signatures — verbal consent logged as audio timestamp in app. Compliant with DPA 2019 oral consent provisions.

02
"Your health story, your ownership" framing

Mother receives a printed card (or SMS summary) stating: her record is portable, she can request a full data export at any time via USSD *384#, her data is never sold individually, and she can withdraw without affecting her care. This framing is not aspirational — it is the consent architecture, and it drives re-enrollment for second pregnancies.

03
Granular opt-in for data uses beyond direct care

Separate opt-in for: (a) anonymised aggregation for county health planning, (b) anonymised use in predictive model training, (c) inclusion in academic research datasets. Default is direct care only. Upsell consent requires a second explicit action — never bundled into initial registration.

04
Withdrawal mechanism

USSD opt-out: *384*0# at any time. Record is frozen (not deleted) per DPA 2019 requirements — mother can request deletion via the county health office. CHP is notified; care continues uninterrupted. Withdrawal rate is a product health metric monitored monthly.

05
SHA alignment (2026 priority)

Kenya's Social Health Authority is building portable health records for every enrolled citizen. Maternanet positions itself as the richest data source a mother can contribute to her SHA profile. When SHA mandates digital ANC records (expected 2026–2027), Maternanet's consent architecture is pre-aligned. First-mover regulatory compliance = 18-month lead over competitors.

Government data nationalisation

A ministry directive could claim that all ANC data generated by CHPs operating under government mandate belongs to the state. Precedent exists in East Africa.

Data quality degradation at scale (14,000+ CHPs)

At scale, bad actors, connectivity failures, and tired workers produce implausible values, fabricated entries, and missing critical fields. Below 80% completeness, records are excluded from model training — but they still look like data.

Automated quality scoring per CHP built into the data pipeline by Month 18 of 2026. Records below 70% completeness flagged and excluded from model training data (not deleted). CHPs below the quality threshold receive re-training, not dismissal. Quality score is a metric on the CHA supervisor dashboard.
